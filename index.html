About Meteors & Asteroids ☄️

Asteroids and meteors are cosmic travelers. Some just pass by, while others come close enough to be monitored by NASA.

Types of Asteroids & Meteors

S-type (Silicaceous)

Made of silicate rock, the most common asteroid type.

C-type (Carbonaceous)

Rich in carbon, very dark, and among the oldest objects.

Meteoroids

Smaller rocky/metallic fragments that can become meteors or meteorites.

Potential Impacts 🌍

Asteroids can have different effects depending on their size, speed, and impact location.

🌠 Atmospheric Entry

Most small asteroids burn up in Earth’s atmosphere, creating shooting stars.

💥 Regional Damage

Medium asteroids can cause massive explosions, damaging cities.

🌊 Tsunamis

Ocean impacts may generate waves that threaten coastal areas.

🌐 Global Extinction

Huge asteroids (>10 km) can cause climate change and wipe out species.

NASA’s Planetary Defense Solutions 🚀

NASA’s mission is to detect, track, and deflect asteroids that could threaten Earth.

🛰️ Planetary Defense Coordination Office (PDCO)

Responsible for finding, tracking, and mitigating potential asteroid threats.

🚀 DART Mission

First mission to change an asteroid’s motion in space — proof that deflection works.

🌌 NEOWISE Mission

Infrared telescope that helps estimate asteroid size, orbit, and composition.

☄️ Sentry System

Automated system that predicts potential asteroid impacts up to 100 years ahead.

💡 Fun Fact

NASA’s DART mission successfully tested asteroid deflection by crashing a spacecraft into an asteroid at 6.6 km/s — proving that humanity can alter an asteroid’s trajectory if needed!
